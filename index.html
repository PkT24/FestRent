— Launch Pipeline
01
Build the Foundation
Month 1–2

No users yet. Design the trust infrastructure that makes you different from every other rental platform. This is your moat.

Design supplier verification pipeline — government ID, background check via Checkr, item photography & valuation
Launch a single waitlist page to capture supplier and organizer emails before app is built
Lock down 5–8 core item categories (power banks, wagons, camping gear, chairs, strollers, shade structures, coolers)
Begin MVP development: event posting, item listing, in-app booking, payments, ratings
Set up insurance layer — per-item coverage for verified rentals
02
Land One Pilot Event
Month 2–3

The most important step. Find one mid-sized local festival (2,000–10,000 attendees) with an approachable organizer. This gives you a concrete hook to recruit suppliers around.

Target community festivals, local music events, food festivals, or college events — not mega events
Pitch organizers: "Free value for your attendees, we handle everything, just list us in your comms"
Secure on-site presence — a small booth or designated pickup zone within the grounds
Get listed in the event's official app, email newsletter, and social media
03
Onboard Verified Suppliers
Month 3–4

Use the confirmed event as your recruitment pitch. You're not asking suppliers to join a platform — you're asking them to earn money at a specific event in 6 weeks.

Post in Facebook Groups, Reddit, Nextdoor, and student communities in your city
Target 20–30 verified suppliers for the pilot — enough to cover all core categories with redundancy
Run each supplier through full verification before approval
Brief suppliers on pickup logistics, pricing guidance, and dispute process
04
Execute the Pilot
Month 4–5

Be physically on the ground. Things will go wrong — treat every failure as data. This weekend generates testimonials, content, and everything you need to pitch the next 10 events.

Staff the on-site hub — at least 2 people from your team present the full event
Handle disputes and issues manually — don't rely on the app alone yet
Capture video, photos, and testimonials from renters and suppliers
Track: what rented most, what sat unsold, what people asked for that wasn't listed
Collect NPS scores from both sides of the marketplace
05
Scale to More Events
Month 5+

Use pilot data and testimonials to land 5–10 more events. Expand supplier base. Refine the app based on real usage. Start exploring organizer partnership deals.

Build a repeatable event onboarding playbook from the pilot learnings
Approach larger events armed with proof — transaction data, testimonials, photos
Introduce organizer partnership tier — revenue share for events that drive high volume
Expand item categories based on pilot demand data
Explore locker/storage infrastructure for contactless pickup at larger events

— Key Risks & Mitigations
🐔
Chicken & Egg Problem
Need events to get suppliers, need suppliers to get events. Classic two-sided marketplace problem.
→ Manually seed supply. Recruit 20–30 suppliers around one confirmed event before launch.
💥
Damaged Items
Renter damages a supplier's wagon or power bank. Without a clear process, trust breaks down fast.
→ Escrow deposits + item photography before/after + insurance coverage for verified listings.
👻
No-Shows
Supplier doesn't show at pickup, or renter ghosts. Ruins experience for both sides.
→ In-app check-in system, automatic cancellation penalties, platform backup inventory for pilot events.
